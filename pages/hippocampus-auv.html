Hippocampus AUV Intern

Hamburg University of Technology · May 2024 – Aug 2024

During this internship, I worked with the Hippocampus research team at the Hamburg University of Technology in Germany. My work was on developing pH and electrical conductivity sensors and their integration onto an AUV to measure the quality of an ecosystem.

01 — Circuitry Design

During development, I had to consider that conductivity sensors output a voltage, while pH sensors measure a voltage, so I developed an isolation interface to prevent interference during operation. This allowed all the sensor voltage measurements to be routed to the same I2C port of a Raspberry Pi before processing the electrical input into its corresponding measurement value.

02 — PCB Design

I used a 4-layer PCB to minimize wiring complications and improve power connection consistency. Test points were added to ensure power connection consistency during the debugging process. The size of the PCBs were minimized for integration into the AUV's capsule with the electrical conductivity sensor being the largest at under ​2.5x5cm.

03 — PH Sensor

In the pH sensor, the circuit had components that made the voltage signal readable by the Raspberry Pi through shifting and multiplying the voltage signal. The inclusion of a low-pass filter at the end served to smooth out the signal. These additions resulted in a sensor with accuracy of up to 0.01.

04 — Electrical Conductivity Sensor

The electrical conductivity sensor required the generation of an AC wave, and after much design iteration, I eventually landed on using a relaxation oscillator to provide a square wave. This simplified the need for external circuitry while still providing a consistent source.

Another major addition to the sensor was the use of an octal SPST switch to provide a 3x larger range of measurable salinities when compared to commercial options. ​

05 — Circuit Fabrication

Due to using 0603 resistors and parts with similar small builds, I reflow soldered the parts on to minimize human error when manufacturing. During testing, there was found to be an issue with an opamp design. Luckily, this issue was easily fixed by cutting and rewiring two traces on the PCB.​

06 — Electrical Conductivity Sensor

On top of writing the code for the sensors, I also integrated ROS into the sensors for remote data accessing and live data plotting. Through this, I could run and calibrate all of the sensors remotely without the use of a direct cable to the sensor boards, providing increased maneuverability during deployment of the AUV.​
