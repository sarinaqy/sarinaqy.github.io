Assistant Engineer

City of Simi Valley · May 2023 – Aug 2023

At the City of Simi Valley, I worked in the Public Works department. My primary focus was on waterlines and pump systems, but I also assisted in applying for grants.

01 — Waterline Replacement Project

My main project with the City of Simi Valley was to develop plans for a waterline replacement project in a residential area. When deciding on a design, I had to take into account how the customers would be affected and how the design would be implemented. I had to minimize water shutoff time and production cost.

I developed multiple plans, with one plan highlining over the existing waterline to minimize installation costs, and with another plan installing the waterline next to the existing waterline to minimize water shutoff time. In each of these plans I had to analyze how the new waterline would interact with existing infrastructure such as gas lines, sewer lines, and power poles.

After a final design was determined, I created the site plans that would be sent to contractors. This site plan not only detailed the location and depth of the new waterline, but also showed connection details with instructions on installation.

02 — Benefit-Cost Analysis

Apart from developing waterline replacement plans, I prepared the Benefit-Cost Analysis portion of a federal grant application for the purchase of emergency generators at pump stations.

I initially researched what info was needed, including the cost of a generator and the population affected by a potential pump shutdown. I collected information on the cost of upkeep and the frequency of the pump station shutting down. Through this, I contacted people within and outside of my organization for the needed information.

Finally, when the information was collected, I utilized a federal benefit-cost analysis toolkit to calculate the benefit-cost ratio. I had to constantly work through glitches in the toolkit, but I was able to work through it and provide an organized benefit-cost analysis.
